## The honest answer

If you can afford to spend more on the grinder than the espresso machine, do that. A cheap grinder with a great machine will always lose to a great grinder with a cheap machine. We've tested both combinations on the bar.

## Three we recommend

**Under £200 — Eureka Mignon Specialita.** Stepless, quiet, holds calibration through a busy week. The dial is fiddly but you'll touch it twice and then leave it alone for months.

**£200–£500 — DF64 Gen 2.** Single-dose, low retention, 64mm flat burrs. The cheapest grinder we trust to give us repeatable shots. Comes with a slightly questionable funnel; replace it.

**£500+ — Niche Zero.** The one we use at home. Conical burrs, near-zero retention, ergonomic enough to leave on the counter without flinching every time you look at it.

## Things to ignore

**Burr coating type, RPM, motor wattage, "AI grind suggestions".** Marketing. The variables that matter are *consistency* (how tight is the particle distribution?) and *retention* (how much old coffee gets stuck inside between doses?).

## What we use on the bar

Mahlkönig E80 Supreme for espresso, EK43 for filter. Combined retail price exceeds our entire cafe fit-out from year one. They're worth it for a 200-cups-a-day operation; they would be ridiculous in a kitchen.
