## A journal for teams that ship accessible products

**Threshold** is a small, independent journal for product designers, engineers, and accessibility leads working on real systems. We write up the patterns that work, the audit traps that don't, and the regulatory shifts that change what "compliant" means in practice.

No newsletters with five tracking pixels. No sponsored "deep dives". No takes on whether AI will replace designers. Just the writing we wish existed when we started doing this work.

## Latest

- **[EAA enforcement: a 12-point pre-launch checklist](/eaa-checklist/)** — the audit list we run before signing off any site that serves EU consumers
- **[WCAG 2.2: what actually changed for builders](/wcag-2-1-vs-2-2/)** — nine new criteria, three of them affect every form on the web
- **[Designing for low-vision readers](/accessible-typography/)** — type scale, line-height, and contrast defaults that go further than "use 16px"

See [all posts](/posts/) or browse [by topic](/tags/).

## What we cover

- **WCAG & EAA** — the criteria, the audit process, the failure modes
- **Inclusive design patterns** — typography, motion, colour, focus
- **Tooling** — what passes (and what flatters) automated audits
- **Case writeups** — what we tried, what worked, what didn't
